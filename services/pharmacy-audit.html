Frequently Asked Questions

Pharmacy Audit FAQs

What is a pharmacy audit?

A pharmacy audit is a structured review of inventory, operations, compliance, sales, documentation, and staff practices.

Who should conduct a pharmacy audit?

New, existing, independent, hospital, and chain pharmacies can benefit from periodic operational and inventory audits.

Can an audit help reduce expired stock?

Yes. The audit can identify expiry risks and recommend better purchasing, stock rotation, monitoring, and return procedures.

Will I receive an audit report?

Yes. The audit includes findings, identified gaps, and practical recommendations for improvement.

How often should a pharmacy be audited?

The frequency depends on the pharmacy size and operational needs, but periodic audits help prevent issues from becoming serious.
